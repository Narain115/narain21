### CATEGORY 1: Semiconductor & Process Engineering

**Project 1: HBM Wafer Yield Investigation**

**Problem Tab:**
- AMD faced critical yield issues with High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) stacks
- 12-layer vertical die stacks, sourcing from multiple suppliers
- Target: 78% yield (0.98^12 = 0.78 probability calculation)
- Actual: Falling short of target
- Challenge: 2,000+ data points per wafer × multiple suppliers × physical locations × test parameters
- Manual RCA took 5+ days per investigation
- By the time problems identified, defective batches already in production
- Millions in scrap costs

**Approach Tab:**
- Built Python visualization pipeline using NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib
- Created spatial heatmaps showing yield distribution across wafer surface
- Pattern discovery: Edge dies at 93% yield vs center at 99-100%
- Contacted supplier for transistor-level measurements across entire wafer
- Root cause traced: Oxygen contamination from Czochralski crystal growth process
- O₂ from quartz crucible dissolves into molten silicon, concentrates at crystal edges
- Higher O₂ at edges creates precipitation sites during thermal processing
- Developed die placement algorithm (NumPy/Pandas) to flag high-risk edge zones before assembly

**Results Tab:**
- 60% reduction in RCA time: 5+ days → under 48 hours
- Recovered >15% yield loss
- Saved millions in scrap costs
- 8% FPY improvement, Cpk 1.6 → 1.8
- Python tool became standard for packaging team

**Technical Details Tab:**
- **Python Pipeline:**
  1. Data extraction from test equipment (2,000+ points/wafer)
  2. Pandas DataFrame construction with die coordinates
  3. NumPy spatial correlation analysis (edge vs center)
  4. Matplotlib/Seaborn heatmap generation (color-coded by yield)
  5. Statistical visualization for trend identification

- **Die Placement Algorithm:**
  - Input: Wafer map with die coordinates + test results
  - Processing: Calculate radial distance from wafer center for each die
  - Threshold: Flag dies beyond 85% of wafer radius as high-risk
  - Output: Exclusion list for assembly process
  - Validation: SPC charts tracking yield improvement over time

- **Czochralski O₂ Contamination Mechanism:**
  Silicon wafers grown using Czochralski process (seed crystal pulled from molten silicon). Oxygen from quartz crucible dissolves into melt and incorporates into crystal. Higher oxygen concentrations at crystal edges (last to solidify) create precipitation sites during subsequent thermal processing, degrading transistor performance. This edge-to-center gradient = 93% vs 99% yield pattern.
